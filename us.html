US Folklore and Myths

In this final section I thought it would be interesting to take a look at some local US folklore and urban legends. Perhaps the most famous being that of Bigfoot or the sasquatch. Appearing in both Canadian and American folklore, the sasquatch is an ape like creature said to inhabit forest environments. The most famous documented evidence for the creature is the Patterson-Gimlin film, a short video taken in 1967 in Northern California by filmmaker Roger Patterson. While many skeptics are quick to claim its a man in a suit, others have keenly noted that based on the physiological makeup of the creature it appeared to be a female and pregnant. That is an impressive amount of nuance if the creature is indeed a man in a suit. Most scientists discounted the existence of bigfoot and consider all theevidence to be misidentification, hoax, and folklore. The Mothman is a creature in West Virginia folklore that has a grotesque figure and glowing red eyes. The creature was first sighted by two young couples on November 15, 1966. The pair of couples were driving past an abandoned World War II munitions plant when they sighted the creature. It immediately gave chase and stalked their car from the sky at an amazing speed. When questioned separately by the local sheriff amazingly all of them gave similar accounts and depictions of the creature. Since then several other sightings of the creature have appeared around the area and the Mothman is now a staple of the town. The Mothman has become a major tourist attraction and several museums and tourist themed stores celebrate the legend. You can actually watch the episode of Buzzfeed unsolved below where Shane and Ryan visit the town and locals and uncover the mystery of the Mothman.

Navajo legends tell the story of the skin-walker. A harmful witch that has the ability to possess, or disguise themselves as animals. Animals associated with such creatures include coyotes and wolves. There is a tract of land today known as skinwalker ranch in which the owners reportedly sight the creatures a couple times each year. Today Skinwalkers remain an integral part of American mythology.
